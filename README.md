Hitotose – The 24 Solar Terms & 72 Microseasons

Living gently with the rhythm of the seasons.

In traditional Japanese culture, the year was not only divided into four seasons, but further refined into 24 solar terms (Nijūshi-sekki) and 72 microseasons (Shichijūni-kō). Each microseason reflects a subtle change in nature—such as the first sprouting grasses, the songs of insects, or the ripening of crops. These divisions were once closely connected to everyday life, agriculture, and the way people observed the natural world.

Hitotose is an app designed to help you notice those quiet seasonal shifts.

Through photographs inspired by the imagery of the 72 microseasons, the app introduces seasonal elements such as fish, vegetables, and flowers that are at their peak at that time of year. It also presents traditional Japanese colors associated with each season, along with simple color coordination ideas that can be enjoyed in daily life.

Amid the pace of modern life, Hitotose offers a small moment to pause and breathe with the seasons.

If it helps you gently feel the turning of the year, then it has fulfilled its purpose.

Hitotose brings you a quiet sense of time lived together with the seasons.
